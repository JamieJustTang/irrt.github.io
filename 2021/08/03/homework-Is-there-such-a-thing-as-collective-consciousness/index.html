Homework | Is there such a thing as "collective consciousness"?

2021-08-03

Homework | Answer a 500-word essay on ONE of the following questions.

Is there such a thing as ‘collective consciousness’?

 

I don’t think there is a thing as ‘collective consciousness’ on realism level. I’m an ironist of scientific realism, which means I’m inspired by those realism hypothesis and the practicability of models constructed by that hypothesis without being stubbornly authentic to any one of them. Below I’d like to clarify the practicability of ‘collective consciousness’ as well as its illusion-like doubtful features in my eyes.

The word ‘collective consciousness’ appeared 39 times in the text of The Division. In Chapter 2, Mechanical Solidarity, or Solidarity by Similarities, Durkheim wrote,

Since the terms ‘collective’ and ‘social’ are often taken as synonyms, one is inclined to believe that the collective consciousness is the entire social consciousness, that is, co-terminous with the psychological life of society, whereas, particularly in higher societies, it constitutes only a very limited part of it.(p39)

Such an expression needs to be put into the background of Spencer’s theory of social organism in order to make sense. Thus, the presupposition of theory of social organism is also that of ‘collective consciousness’ – society is an organism, having its living system and psychological activities. Then the question might become: is there such a thing as ‘social organism’?

Can we point out a thing in reality and call it as ‘social organism’? We can’t, but only point the image of that word in our mind. And it’s easy to understand that this image is constructed by our imagination rather than witnessed with our own eyes. Also, Gods, Devils, nationality, love… words like these are similar with ‘social organism’, one may truly believe that they exist but can never point out their shapes in reality.

However, there does exist something that can convince one of their ‘existence’. Here my views are somehow empiricism – the trace convinces people that a thing exists and shows how it is. Such a cognitive logic is showed in the primitive ontology and causality theory – Where there’s reek, there’s heat. It also gives a critique to them – any discussion transcends the trace (or, experience) does no help with causality explanation.

Thus, pure discussion about the existence of ‘social organism’ or so-called ‘collective consciousness’ have no help, unless we trace back to the trace of ‘collective consciousness’. According to my classification, the phrase ‘collective consciousness’ appeared in 9 kinds of situations, which are below:

  1. Theory of organism and holism of society
  2. Force of morality – crimes, offence to collective consciousness
  3. Society structure of collective consciousness: authority – repressive law
  4. Individual consciousness (and its distinction from collective consciousness)
  5. Restitutory law and repressive law
  6. Solidarity: mechanical solidarity and organic solidarity
  7. Division of labour: the development from ancient times to modern times
  8. construction and variance of individual
  9. (other) collective consciousness

Limited by space, a summary of those texts won’t be given. Yet we’ve already been able to draw a general map of the meanings of “collective consciousness”*.* To confirm the existence of “collective consciousness” is equal to confirm the practical (pragmatic is better, though) meaning of those sentences and the truth of social facts[1] related. In short, I believe many of those paragraphs do make sense, and give me new inspirations and understanding to society, morality, history…etc.
